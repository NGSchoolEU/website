Welcome to NGSchool2025: Sequencing Toolbox for Computational Biologists, the 7th edition of our renowned summer school series!

This year's program is dedicated to equipping students, early-career scientists, and professionals with cutting-edge skills in sequencing technologies and their applications in computational biology, bioinformatics, and biomedicine.

About NGSchool2025

Sequencing technologies have revolutionized the biological sciences, allowing researchers to gain unprecedented insights into the genetic and molecular underpinnings of life. With the rise of next-generation sequencing (NGS) and long-read sequencing technologies, scientists can now uncover complex genetic variations, structural rearrangements, and epigenetic modifications that were previously undetectable. These advancements are transforming fields like personalized medicine, agriculture, and environmental science, with far-reaching implications for global health and sustainability.

At NGSchool2025, you'll have the chance to learn from experts in the field, engage in hands-on workshops, and collaborate with like-minded peers from around the world. Our program is tailored to the educational needs of participants from the Visegrád Four (V4) and Central and Eastern Europe (CEE) regions but welcomes aspiring computational biologists and bioinformaticians from all backgrounds.

Key Topics

Our curriculum covers a wide array of topics, ranging from fundamental sequencing techniques to advanced computational approaches. Here's a sneak peek at what to expect:

  • Base Modifications & Long-Read Sequencing Workshop
    Delve into cutting-edge long-read technologies like Nanopore sequencing, explore how base modifications impact genome architecture, and discover the latest analysis methods.
  • De-Novo Transcript Discovery & Algorithmics
    Learn how to assemble and annotate transcriptomes from scratch using state-of-the-art algorithms for long-read sequencing data.
  • Genomic Architecture & Structural Variations
    Understand the role of large-scale genomic rearrangements in shaping species diversity and their implications for human disease.
  • CRISPR Experimental Design & Analysis
    Explore the transformative potential of CRISPR-Cas systems for genome editing, and gain hands-on experience designing experiments and analyzing CRISPR data.
  • Immunology: TCR/BCR Sequencing
    Dive into immune system genetics with T-cell receptor (TCR) and B-cell receptor (BCR) sequencing, exploring their applications in immunology and disease.
  • Metagenomics for Virus Diagnosis & Surveillance
    Investigate how metagenomics is being used for real-time virus detection and monitoring, with a special focus on diagnostic and epidemiological applications.
  • Population Genomics
    Discover how population-scale genetic studies are reshaping our understanding of human evolution, adaptation, and diversity.
  • Cancer Evolution
    Explore the genetic changes driving cancer progression and resistance, and learn how sequencing technologies are helping to chart the course of cancer evolution.
  • Single-Cell RNA & Spatial Transcriptomics
    Gain insights into the rapidly advancing fields of single-cell sequencing and spatial transcriptomics, which allow researchers to map gene expression at unprecedented resolution.

Registration fee

The registration fee for the selected participants is 180 EUR/820 PLN for academia and 400 EUR/1840 PLN for industry. This fully covers the costs of accommodation and boarding during the school.
We try to make our events accessible and affordable for all and keep the registration fees to a minimum. However, we understand that in some circumstances covering the costs of participation in our event can still be challenging. We do not want money to be a limiting factor in anyone's participation, so we will provide financial support for several selected participants, primarily based on their socioeconomic status. We can offer two types of support:

  • Travel grant to help you cover the cost of your travel to the event's venue.
  • Fee discount or waiver directed to those participants who cannot get the fee covered by their institutions.

Both types of support will be preferentially grated to participants from academia (students, PhD candidates and faculty stuff), but application from industry will be considered, too. If you wish to be considered for travel grant, fee discount/waiver or both, make sure to fill the Bursary section of the application form, explaining your situation. We will try our best to help you.

For participants applying from Russian institutions

NGSchool condemns the aggression against Ukraine in the strongest possible terms and stands in solidarity with our colleagues in Ukraine. Since Russian Universities publicly supported the war we are unable to accept participants affiliated with those institutions. However, we note that many Russian academics, at great personal peril, have publicly criticized this invasion. We would like to support all those harmed by Putin's regime and therefore extend our invitation to Russian academics and students provided they will come as individuals, without affiliation. We will not be able to accept payments for registration fees from Russian universities and companies and will not be able to provide receipts to those organizations.
